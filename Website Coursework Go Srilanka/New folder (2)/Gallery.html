Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is the best known tea-growing district of Sri Lanka with her impressive lush green tea plantations. It has so many beautiful tea estates because the conditions like soil, climate, etc, are formidable.When you travel Sri Lanka, you can't miss exploring its incredible tea plantations and learn about tea production. So when you're in Nuwara Eliya, then you should definitely explore the Nuwara Eliya tea plantations.

Gregory Lake is a wetland converted into a beautiful lake. Used for recreational purposes, the lake is visited by large number of tourists every year. In there we can do boat rodes, cycling and horse rides. The beauty of this lake will leave you totally mesmerized. Also, you can take beautiful pictures as a memories.

Ambewela is a small town in Nuwara Eliya district popularly known for its 2 dairy farms called “Ambewela Farm” and “New Zealand Farm”. Only the New Zealand Farm is open to visitors out of the two. the farms spans over 1500 acres of green pastures spreading as far as the eye can see dotted with brown/black and white cows twice the size of the average cow in Sri Lanka. Visitors can can go in to the sheds where huge stud bulls are kept, rabbit breading areas, cow sheds and also you can visit the automated milking process of the farm.

Lipton's Seat or Lipton Seat is a viewpoint situated in the Haputale mountain range, atop the Dambatenne Tea Estate. It is one of the popular view point. Located at a height of 1970 m above sea level, Lipton's Seat is a beautiful tourist attraction that offers awe-inspiring views of the surrounding verdant hills and tea plantations. From the summit, one will have a bird's eye view of the Uva, Sabaragamuwa, Central and Eastern provinces. On a clear day, one should be able to spot the Handapanagala Lake, the Chandrika Lake and the Udawalawe Lake as well as the Wedihiti Kanda mountain range. This place is also perfect to witness the best of sunrise and sunset.

Historical places

The Jetavanaramaya is a stupa located in the ruins of Jetavana in the sacred world heritage city of Anuradhapura, Sri Lanka. A part of a sash or belt tied by the Buddha is believed to be the relic that is enshrined here. The structure is significant in the island's history for it represents the tensions within the Theravada and Mahayana sects of Buddhism; it is also significant in recorded history as one of the tallest structures in the ancient world; making it the tallest stupa in the ancient world. the engineering ingenuity behind the construction of the structure is a significant development in the history of the island.

Sigiriya is one of the most valuable historical monuments of Sri Lanka. Referred by locals as the Eighth Wonder of the World, this ancient palace and fortress complex has significant archaeological importance. The fortress complex includes remnants of a ruined palace, surrounded by an extensive network of fortifications, vast gardens, ponds, canals, alleys, and fountains. The term Sigiriya originates from the word Sihagri, Lion Rock.

Initially built by the Portuguese in the 16th century during their conquests, the fort was later fortified and conquered by the Dutch in the 17th century, until it later fell to the might of the British. This fortress is completed with 14 bastions which is still the best-preserved and best example for the fortified city in Asia built by Europeans by showing the mix of European architectural styles and South Asian traditions. The old town of Galle was once used as a trading port for spices and other goods for over 200 years. However, today, the fort has been transformed into a place of history, romance.

Parakramabahu I, also known as Parakramabahu the Great, was a king of Sri Lanka who reigned from the kingdom of Polonnaruwa. Parakrama Samudraya, the massive reservoir built by king Parakramabahu is surrounded by a large amount of ancient ruins of this ancient kingdom. There are remains of some of the walls and steps of this palace in addition to the base platform even after 850+ years.

The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic is a world-renowned place of worship, where the left tooth of Gautama Buddha is enshrined. was named as a world heritage by UNESCO in 1988. The architecture is of unique Kandyan architectural style with a combination of the unique style used to build “Dalada Mandira” the shrines which housed the Sacred Tooth Relic previously in other kingdoms. The famous Kandy Lake also known as “Kiri Muhuda” to the South and “Natha & Paththini Devala” on the West. The temple is adorned with intricate carvings using gold, silver, bronze and ivory.

Dambulla Rock, about 600m above the plains, is a place where the sun shines well. There is a cave complex in this rock. There is a stone temple of ancient Buddhist value in Sri Lanka. This temple is named as Jambukola. It is said that the temple was known as Swarnagiri Cave. Today it is known as Rangiri Dambulla Viharaya.


Beaches

Mirissa is among the popular beach destinations in the south of Sri Lanka. The region is full of stunning Sri Lankan beaches, whale watching, surfing waves, and fantastic pubs and restaurants. Mirissa has been growing fast over the past few years and also becoming busier. It is a beautiful beach, with a relaxed and laidback atmosphere.

The beaches in Hikkaduwa have been attracting tourists.The beach stretches on spanning over kilometers and hosts many fun activities to keep the tourists engaged. The clear waters beckon the nature lovers who wish to take a dip inside the waters to witness the vibrant, colorful aquatic life.The coral sanctuary hosts gorgeous coral formations, which house a variety of fishes and large turtles. Hikkaduwa is famous for serving delicious seafood curries, relaxing massages, Buddhist temples, and watersports such as surfing, snorkeling, and diving.

Pasikuda to the north of Batticaloa has become the most important beach resort at Sri Lanka's East Coast. Pasikudah Beach is perfect for such a tourism development project, because it is the safe for swimming. The bay is protected by a reef and the water is shallow.

The famous beach facing open space between the old Colonial Ceylon Parliament building now used as the Presidential Secretariat Building and the elegant colonial Colombo Galle Face Hotel is known as Galle Face. The open green space of Galle Face is about 1/2 km long. It is the biggest open public space in the congested capital city. Because of the constant breeze coming in from the Indian Ocean kite flying is a popular sport.

Nilaweli beach is the spectacular beach in Trincomalee City boasts of sandy shores and clear waters. Apart from lazing around, there are plenty of things you can do here to have a fun-filled excursion by the beach. Nilaveli means the 'Moon shine over the land or open space' in the Tamil language.There are plenty of things to do in this beach.they are Scuba Diving, Boat Tours, Sunbathing on the beach, Sightseeing.

Unawatuna is one of the biggest tourist destinations in Sri Lanka and is the most “famous” beach in the country. It is a lovely banana-shaped beach of golden sand and turquoise water, surrounded by green palm trees!


Water Falls and Mountains

Dunhinda is a very popular waterfall in Sri Lanka. It is a remarkable waterfall when considering the beauty of it. The water resembles a thin cloud as it cascades 63m downwards into a large pool. The backdrop to the fall is known as 'Dunhinda Adaviya'. The name Dunhinda derived from the misty appearance in front of the waterfall during the rainy season. This access path to the waterfall is open from 7 AM to 5 PM. Halfway down the walking path, you will come across another waterfall below the main waterfall. This called Kuda Dunhinda Falls.

Diyaluma Falls in Sri Lanka, is the second-tallest waterfall in the country and a stunning spectacle of nature. The enormous rocky facade is peppered with lush, green vegetation, thriving in the mist of the falls. Up top, the ceaseless flow of water has carved intricate channels and pools into the rock, weaving through the verdant mountain terrain. These pools are amongst the best in the world for a scenic swim, with views that will take your breath away.

Bambarakanda is the highest waterfall in Sri Lanka. This is not a wide waterfall but drops as a thin line from a rocky outcrop. The fall is formed by the Uduweriya Haputale mountain and Kuda Oya, a tributary of Walawe River.

The Knuckles Mountain Range also called the Knuckles Massif; a bio-diversity hotspot it consists of five peaks; namely Kirigalpottha, Gombaniya, Knuckles, Koboneelagala and Dotulugala which form the shape of the knuckles in a clenched fist. The climate here varies from hot to very wet and cold with some higher elevations reporting ground frost during January-March with strong winds at most other times. The Forest Reserve has recorded up of 1033 plant species belonging to 141 families of which 15% are endemic. In addition there are 128 bird species of which 17 are endemic; There are 60 species of butterflies. There are also 17 species of mollusks and 53 species of reptiles.

Pidurutalagala Mountain in Sri Lanka is the tallest mountain on the island. It is also the only ultra prominent peak on the island. An ultra prominent peak is a mountain that has topographic prominence. The mountain of Pidurutalagala has a forest reserve surrounding it. The area of the mountain and forest reserve has been designated an ultra-high security zone due to the summit being used to house the communications array of the Sri Lankan Government and the Armed Forces of the country. Unlike most mountain summits which are rocky, Pidurutalagala Mountain in Sri Lanka had a cap of pale green vegetation on its somewhat flat summit.

Namunukula is one of Sri Lanka's 12 highest mountains and by far the tallest peak in the east of Sri Lanka's southern mountain range, which stretches from Siri Pada in the west Namunukula in the east. Namunukula is the most prominent peak of this Uva tea region. The Sinhalese name "Namunukula" means "Nine Peaks" and refers to the entire range.


Forests and parks

Sinharaja is a Rain Forest and a biodiversity hotspot and wet zone Sri Lanka. Sinharaja rain forest is the country’s last viable area of primary tropical rain forest. More than 60% of the trees are endemic to Sinharaja Forest and many of them are considered rare. Sinharaja Forest was recognized as being the only considerable patch of virgin tropical rain forest in the Island. Sinharaja Rain Forest is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Sinharaja is bounded by rivers on three sides.

The primary purpose of the orphanage has been to provide a lifeline to the orphaned baby elephants and adult elephants lost in the wilderness. The elephants at the Orphanage aren’t subjected to any form of stress, abuse or threat at all and are supported by a team of employees numbering to over 100 including a group of mahouts. In there, the main attraction is clearly to observe the elephants bathing which is quite a spectacle.

Udawalawe National Park was established on 30th June 1972. Primary objective was to protect the immediate catchment areas and to provide a safe refuge for displaced wild mainly Elephants as a result of the Walawe Basin Development project. Udawalawe is the third most visited national park and an important habitat for water birds and Sri Lankan elephants

Wilpattu is Sri Lanka's oldest and largest national park which island's premier wildlife viewing destination. Wilpattu National Park was declared as a sanctuary in 1905 and upgraded to a National Park on February 25, 1938. When consider about the landscape of the park it is flat and covered with dry evergreen forest and deciduous thorn scrub. Further Wilpattu National Park is an ideal place for wildlife educational field trips & research in Sri Lanka.

Kanneliya, Dediyagala and Nakiyadeniya also known as KDN together form a tropical lowland rain forest complex making it the last large remaining rain forest after the famous Sinharaja Rain Forest. one of the places with the finest distribution of plants in the whole of South Asia. It was designated as a biosphere reserve in 2004 by UNESCO. December to March provides you a good opportunity see resident, endemic and migratory birds at Kanneliya Rain Forest. The Kanneliya National Park made up of dense, less dense and plantation forests are a biodiversity hotspot with its wet evergreen vegetation that is home to many species of endemic wildlife.
